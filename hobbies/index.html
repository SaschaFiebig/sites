Metalpoint:

Metalpoint-drawings are something else and kind of special in my opinion. Particular Silverpoint drawings are defining for the renaissance art. The drawing ground has to be prepared for the Silver/Metalpoint to take hold. Today, this is mostly done using acrylic ground to prepare the drawing surface. Back in the olden day's artist's used a binder mixed with ash and/or bone powders. Over time the metals used to draw oxidize and change there color. Silver takes on a sepia patina and copper a greenish-brown-yellow tone. Some other metals only change hue. The one exception to that particular phenomena is our good old friend Aurum (Lat. for Gold). The other thing about gold is, that drawings appear to be gray in color, only when you see light reflection at an angle you can see the golden sheen from the drawing.

My favorite painting media

Oil:

Later in live I started to be more and more interested in painting, oil painting in particular made me very curious. DaVinci made oil painting a trent, before that egg-tempera was the medium to use. Painting is a lot more complex than drawing. You have to prepare the painting ground so your painting will withstand the test of time. You have to go by the rules of fat over lean, so the painting doesn't crack to much. Alas, the final layer of varnish must be applied. Modern varnishes are removable and allow for easier restoration.
